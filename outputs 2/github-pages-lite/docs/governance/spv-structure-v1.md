# Sahajeevanam Puram – SPV Structure Note (V1)

## Purpose

This note outlines a workable concept-stage SPV and governance structure for Sahajeevanam Puram – Advaita-Loka.

## 1. Why an SPV Is Needed

The project cuts across:

- land and planning,
- accessibility and social welfare,
- infrastructure,
- digital systems,
- education,
- health,
- employment,
- and capital formation.

A standard department-led model is likely to fragment ownership. An SPV structure can provide:

- unified project stewardship,
- phased capital and partner management,
- clearer accountability,
- protected governance architecture,
- implementation continuity.

## 2. Recommended Role of the SPV

The SPV should be responsible for:

- project planning and coordination,
- feasibility and DPR commissioning,
- land and regulatory coordination,
- partner selection and contracting,
- capital stack assembly,
- standards and governance framework oversight,
- phase-gated implementation review.

## 3. Suggested Ownership Logic

Illustrative concept-stage structure:

- majority public anchor or state-linked control during early stages,
- PPP and strategic participation through project or subsidiary layers,
- ring-fenced mission clauses around accessibility and governance safeguards.

## 4. Governance Layers

### Board

Should include:

- state nominees,
- infrastructure and finance experts,
- accessibility experts,
- independent governance members,
- technology and public-systems expertise.

### Advisory Council

Non-board but high-value participation from:

- disability rights leaders,
- elder-care and education experts,
- urban planning experts,
- research and institutional partners.

### Accessibility Council

A protected review body with real authority to:

- assess exclusion risk,
- flag discriminatory systems,
- review public-realm and digital-interface design,
- issue design and operations recommendations.

## 5. Operating Structure

Suggested internal workstreams:

- planning and development,
- finance and capital,
- digital systems and Mopus City-OS,
- accessibility and resident systems,
- partnerships and external relations,
- legal, governance, and audit.

## 6. Subsidiary / Project Layer Logic

As the project matures, some workstreams may sit in separate delivery entities or PPP structures:

- housing and construction,
- mobility,
- digital platform services,
- education and Lyceum operations,
- commercial and export activities.

This allows capital and risk to be allocated more precisely without weakening core governance.

## 7. Capital Structure Logic

Concept-stage target structure:

- state or SPV support anchor,
- PPP participation,
- green and social bond structures where appropriate,
- catalytic / development finance,
- CSR and grant participation.

## 8. Key Safeguards

- privacy by design,
- explicit consent for biometric-linked systems,
- audit rights,
- accessibility review requirements,
- transparent standards publication,
- phase-gated financial approval.

## 9. Immediate Next Step

The first governance deliverable should be a Phase 0 SPV design note covering:

- legal pathway options,
- role of state entities,
- board structure,
- authority boundaries,
- fund-flow principles,
- and approval triggers for later phases.
