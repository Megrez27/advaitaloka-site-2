# Sahajeevanam Puram Roadmap

## Phase 0: Clarify the Foundation

Objective: Turn the vision into a coherent strategic package for Sahajeevanam Puram – Advaita-Loka.

Outputs:

- core narrative,
- positioning language,
- project website,
- concept note,
- pilot framing,
- stakeholder map.

Success looks like:

- a new person can understand the project in under five minutes,
- the idea can be presented credibly to partners or backers,
- the first prototype paths are clearly defined.

## Phase 1: Define the Pilot

Objective: Choose the first real thing we can build.

Possible pilot tracks:

1. Digital pilot:
   a resident platform, mobility interface, mentor network, or City OS prototype.
2. Spatial pilot:
   a sample zero-barrier home, block, or experience center.
3. Program pilot:
   a live mentorship and accessibility workforce program with a small community.
4. Policy pilot:
   a formal proposal for government, SPV framing, regulatory autonomy, and implementation model.

Success looks like:

- one pilot is selected,
- scope is narrow enough to deliver,
- the pilot proves something essential rather than trying to prove everything.

## Phase 2: Build the Demonstration Layer

Objective: Create tangible proof.

Outputs may include:

- clickable product prototypes,
- visual masterplan concepts,
- operating model diagrams,
- financial assumptions model,
- brand deck and stakeholder presentation,
- case for pilot funding or institutional partnership.

Success looks like:

- there is something concrete to show, test, and improve,
- early feedback reveals where the concept is strong and where it needs revision.

## Phase 3: Partnership and Validation

Objective: Test the project against reality.

Workstreams:

- accessibility review,
- urban and architectural feasibility,
- legal and governance analysis,
- funding and delivery model,
- resident and community consultation,
- technology stack feasibility.

Success looks like:

- major risks are named early,
- claims become evidence-backed,
- partners can see their role in the ecosystem.

## Phase 4: Launch the First Implementation

Objective: Move from proposal to execution.

Possible scope:

- small campus,
- experience center,
- resident services pilot,
- digital platform rollout through Mopus City-OS,
- mentorship and work network.

Success looks like:

- the first implementation is operational,
- residents or users receive actual value,
- the project starts generating trust, data, and momentum.

## Immediate Priorities

These are the smartest next moves from where we are today:

1. Finalize the base narrative and visual identity.
2. Create a stronger website and public-facing story.
3. Choose one pilot path instead of keeping everything equally abstract.
4. Draft a serious concept note or deck for stakeholders.
5. Define the digital platform architecture at a high level.

## Recommended First Pilot

The strongest starting point is likely a combined narrative + digital demonstration:

- public website,
- system map,
- resident journey flows,
- City OS concept screens,
- pilot proposal deck.

This is faster, cheaper, and more useful right now than pretending the full province is ready for execution.
