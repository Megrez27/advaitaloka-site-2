# Sahajeevanam Puram – Advaita-Loka

## Investor One-Pager (V1)

## What It Is

An AI-enabled accessibility township and inclusive innovation zone proposed for Uttar Pradesh, designed to combine zero-barrier infrastructure, autonomous mobility, digital public systems, and revenue-generating inclusion models.

## Why It Matters

The project sits at the intersection of:

- accessible infrastructure,
- aging and care innovation,
- disability inclusion,
- digital public systems,
- workforce creation,
- place-based economic development.

## Core Thesis

Accessibility and care are usually framed as public cost centers. Sahajeevanam Puram reframes them as long-term economic assets through:

- lower-friction infrastructure,
- inclusion-linked productivity,
- exportable standards and IP,
- digital workforce participation,
- mentorship and education revenues,
- platform and operating-system services.

## Market Logic

The model is relevant where there is:

- a large underserved population,
- high infrastructure deficit,
- need for state-backed demonstration,
- and potential to create exportable inclusion standards.

## Revenue Streams

- digital workforce services,
- Lyceum / mentorship and education programs,
- inclusive design IP licensing,
- platform and operating-system implementation services.

## Capital Story

### Concept-stage development envelope

- approximately INR 5,200 crore

### Immediate capital focus

- Phase 0 ask:
  INR 22 crore

This makes the first investment decision smaller, more legible, and feasibility-linked.

## Why It Could Be Investable

- differentiated narrative with public relevance,
- multiple monetization pathways,
- strong policy alignment,
- potential flagship positioning,
- high signaling value if state-backed,
- exportable standards opportunity.

## Key Risks

- land and regulatory complexity,
- execution intensity,
- revenue timing mismatch,
- overreach in branding or legal framing,
- need for disciplined phased delivery.

## What Happens Next

1. Phase 0 authorization and structuring
2. demand, land, and feasibility validation
3. demonstration-layer buildout
4. pilot selection and partner onboarding
5. capital formation for implementation phases
